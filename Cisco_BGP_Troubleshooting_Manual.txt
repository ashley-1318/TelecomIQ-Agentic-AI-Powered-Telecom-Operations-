[SECTION 4.2] RECOVERING FROM FATAL BGP PACKET CORRUPTION & BACKHAUL FIBER CUTS
Symptom: Edge Tower dropping large volumes of traffic. Physical Layer 1 (Fiber) cut detected causing routing loops. BGP neighbors transition to Idle/Active but fail to reach Established state.

Immediate Mitigation Steps for Tier 3 NOC Engineers (Tower A9 overrides):

1. SSH INITIATION
Access the primary edge router via secure shell on port 22.

2. FLUSH BGP ROUTING TABLE
A hard flush is required to clear corrupt packets and reset the TCP session for the BGP peers. Do NOT use hard reset in business hours unless traffic loss exceeds 80%.
> clear ip bgp * soft

3. PORT ISOLATION
Identify the damaged fiber port (Typically GigabitEthernet0/0/0 for macro-cells). Shut down the port to kill the looping corrupted traffic and force the router to failover to the microwave backup link.
> conf t
> int gi0/0/0
> shut
> no shut

4. VERIFICATION
Run a ping test to the overarching core switch to verify 0% packet loss and confirm BGP neighbor adjacency is re-established.
> do ping 8.8.8.8
> show ip bgp summary

WARNING: Failure to execute these commands in precise order may result in cascading routing table collapses across the regional subnet.
